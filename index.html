Rachel Whiteread

23 December 2017

One Sunday in December, we had forty minutes to spare before the shops on Piccadilly opened. More than enough time to visit Rachel Whiteread’s exhibition at the Tate Britain, I thought. But how wrong I was.

This beautiful, big space was a haven from the chaos of Christmas shoppers and festive season tourists. Being able to roam freely around art of this kind, barely encountering another person, is a privilege in a city like London. Even more, we found delight in trying to work out how the sculptures had been cast and what they had been cast from. There was much to provoke our thoughts and conversation.
